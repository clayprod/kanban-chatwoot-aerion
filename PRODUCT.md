# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Aerion's commercial team, sales managers, SDRs, and analysts who work inside a CRM-style operating system for pipeline management, revenue tracking, lead qualification, PNCP/licitation opportunities, and sales playbooks.

Users are typically in an active work session: checking pipeline status, moving opportunities through a kanban flow, reviewing company or CNPJ data, analyzing public procurement opportunities, and coordinating next actions.

## Product Purpose

The product centralizes Aerion's sales operation into a focused CRM dashboard. It helps the team understand pipeline health, prioritize opportunities, manage follow-up, track goals, and keep procurement-related commercial work connected to daily sales workflows.

Success means users can quickly decide what needs attention, trust the data on screen, and complete routine CRM tasks without feeling like they are navigating a generic website.

## Brand Personality

Operational, commercial, and precise.

The interface should feel like a serious sales CRM: task-focused, data-dense where useful, confident in hierarchy, and consistent across screens. It should support repeated daily use rather than make a marketing impression.

## Anti-references

This should not look like a generic public website, landing page, or decorative web template. Avoid hero-page composition, marketing cards, ornamental sections, stock-site styling, vague SaaS decoration, and layouts that prioritize visual novelty over workflow clarity.

Avoid making CRM actions feel like brochure navigation. The product should feel closer to a professional sales command center than a general web page.

## Design Principles

1. Prioritize workflow over spectacle: every visual decision should help users scan, compare, decide, or act.
2. Make status and priority obvious: pipeline stage, risk, urgency, owner, and next action should be readable at a glance.
3. Preserve CRM familiarity: use standard controls, tables, filters, tabs, modals, and kanban behavior unless a different pattern clearly improves the task.
4. Keep density disciplined: show enough information for commercial judgment without turning screens into undifferentiated text walls.
5. Build trust through consistency: shared components, labels, colors, and states should behave the same across the system.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA as the baseline for contrast, keyboard access, focus states, and readable UI text. Support reduced motion preferences, avoid color-only status communication, and keep dense CRM screens usable for long work sessions.
